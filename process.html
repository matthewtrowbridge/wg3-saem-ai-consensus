Proposed pre-conference consensus process

This is a proposal, not a plan. The goal is to give the group something concrete to react to so we can align on method before we start executing.

The situation

WG3 needs to produce a consensus competency framework for AI in EM education and deliver it as a 2-page summary (May 12), a conference presentation (May 21), and an AEM E&T manuscript (Aug 31).

We are not starting from scratch. At SAEM25, a group of EM faculty conducted a nominal group exercise that produced four themes and ten subthemes describing what emergency physicians need to know about AI. A planned Delphi validation round was never completed. Carl has since developed 14 clinical scenarios. We have mapped scenarios against themes and identified seven candidate gaps. The question: how do we turn this into a validated, publishable framework before May 21?

What we're proposing

A single, well-designed consensus round: one asynchronous survey, one synchronous debrief, then synthesis. Not a formal two-round Delphi, but more rigorous than a discussion.

Step 1 · async

Structured survey

~30 min per person. Four sections: scenario validation, theme assessment, persona test, open-ended. Goes out after April 15; one week to complete.

Step 2 · sync

Group debrief

1-hour video call, WG3 only. Carl and Christian lead. Review results, resolve disagreements, make framework decisions.

Step 3 · async

Revised framework

Matt updates the working site. Co-leads draft 2-page summary. Framework goes to conference as a stable artifact, not a blank slate.

The survey in detail

Sample questions shown below so you can react to the substance, not just the structure.

Section A · ~15 min

Are these the right scenarios?

For each of Carl's 14 clinical scenarios, rate:

Example: "You are given an EKG mid-shift, and the AI interpretation reads 'Brugada syndrome.'"

  • How likely is this in your current or near-future practice? (1-5)
  • How important is it that EM clinicians are prepared? (1-5)
  • What competency does this most demand? (free text)

Purpose: Validate which scenarios are worth building competencies around. Surface competency language in participants' own words.

Section B · ~10 min

Do the themes hold up?

For each of the four SAEM25 themes, evaluate:

Example: Theme T3: Interacting with AI clinically

  • Does this theme cover the competencies the highest-priority scenarios demand? (Yes / Partially / No)
  • If not, what's missing? (free text)
  • Specific enough to guide curriculum development? (Specific enough / Needs refinement / Too vague)

Then for each of the seven candidate gap themes:

  • Should this become a standalone theme? (Yes / Fold into existing / Not needed)

Purpose: Complete the validation the 2025 group planned but never did. Evaluate gap themes.

Section C · ~5 min

Does it matter who the clinician is?

A question we want to test: do competency priorities change based on professional role?

Consider three EM physicians:

  • Community ED doc working shifts for an employed group
  • Academic clinician-educator with residency teaching responsibilities
  • Academic researcher focused on AI/informatics

For each, pick the five most important competencies from the combined theme list.

Purpose: If all three get the same top five, the framework doesn't need a persona axis. If they diverge, it does.

Section D · ~5 min

What are we missing?

Two open-ended questions:

  • "What is the single most important thing WG3's output should say that hasn't been said in published frameworks?"
  • "What should WG3 explicitly leave out of scope?"

Purpose: Capture high-value signal structured questions miss. Surface scope boundaries.

How this maps to the grant

The R13 grant describes a "compressed two-round modified Delphi." Our adaptation: Round 1 = the SAEM25 nominal group (already done; themes generated through structured brainstorming and transcript coding). Round 2 = our asynchronous survey + debrief (themes validated, extended with scenario analysis and gap identification, finalized through group discussion). Conference day = refinement with a broader audience. This is a defensible interpretation that produces documented consensus thresholds for the AEM manuscript.

What we need on April 15

Not decisions — reactions:

  1. Does this process make sense? Are we over-engineering or under-engineering it?
  2. Are the survey questions the right questions? What would you change?
  3. Is the persona dimension worth testing? Or is it a distraction?
  4. Are there scenarios missing? The April 6 call surfaced ambient scribing, patient-brought AI, and metacognition/reflective practice — none in Carl's 14. Should we add them?
  5. Timeline reality check. Can we get the survey out within a day of April 15? If not, what shifts?
