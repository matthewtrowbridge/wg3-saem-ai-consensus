## How We're Handling the Literature Review

We're doing this differently than past SAEM consensus conferences — and it's intentional.

Instead of asking each group to spend weeks screening abstracts through Covidence, the planning committee will provide each working group with an **AI-generated evidence brief**. These are produced using AI-assisted literature synthesis, then reviewed by our research librarian and the planning committee before they reach you.

There's a certain appropriateness to this approach for a conference on AI in medicine. We're using the technology we've convened to study.

---

## What Each Evidence Brief Includes

Each brief will contain:
- Structured overview of the current evidence landscape for your domain
- Key themes and findings from the existing literature
- Identified gaps where evidence is thin or absent
- Curated reference list of high-impact papers

---

## How to Use the Evidence Brief

Think of the brief as a head start — not the final word. Your group's job is to validate, critique, and build on it. Use it as a starting point for generating research questions rather than treating it as a definitive review.

### At Your Kickoff Meeting (Meeting 1)

Use the evidence brief as a discussion starter:
- What rings true?
- What's missing?
- What surprised you?
- What gaps point toward the most important research questions?

### During Question Development

Draw on the evidence brief alongside:
- Your group members' expertise and domain knowledge
- Suggested topics in your working group description
- Consideration of equity, disparities, and priority populations

---

## Databases Used for Source Literature

- PubMed/Medline
- Web of Science

### Cross-Cutting Themes in All Briefs
- **Social determinants of health** and how AI may differentially affect communities
- **Vulnerable populations** who disproportionately rely on emergency departments

| Step | When |
|------|------|
| Planning committee generates AI-assisted evidence briefs | Before kickoff meetings |
| Librarian review and quality check | Before distribution |
| Briefs distributed to working groups | At or before Meeting 1 |
| Groups validate, annotate, and build on briefs | Meeting 1 through question drafting |
| Briefs inform Delphi question development | Ongoing through Round 1 |
