# Published AI Competency Frameworks for Medical Education
**Prepared for WG3 call prep | April 6, 2026**

---

## Why This Matters for WG3

WG3's central task is "defining the core AI competencies that don't yet exist in standardized form." But several frameworks *do* exist — mostly for UME, mostly general (not EM-specific), and mostly focused on foundational knowledge rather than clinical application. This is the landscape WG3 is building on.

A 2024 scoping review (Tolentino et al.) found only two papers describing comprehensive curriculum frameworks for AI in medicine. Since then, the field has accelerated considerably. The list below captures the key published frameworks as of early 2026.

---

## Key Frameworks

### 1. Triola & Rodman 2025 — Generative AI in Medical Education
**Citation:** Triola MM, Rodman A. Integrating Generative Artificial Intelligence Into Medical Education: Curriculum, Policy, and Governance Strategies. *Academic Medicine*. 2025.

**What it does:** Proposes 6 foundational competency domains for generative AI in UME, plus 3 institutional recommendations (policy, governance, curriculum).

**6 Domains:**
- Fundamental knowledge of AI
- Ethics and regulation
- Application to clinical care and digital health
- Assessing quality and accuracy of AI outputs
- Data privacy and security
- Professionalism

**WG3 relevance:** Direct alignment with WG3's scope. The 6 domains could serve as a starting scaffold for Delphi question categories. However, this framework is UME-focused and does not address GME, practicing clinician CPD, or faculty development — all within WG3's scope. It also doesn't address EM-specific applications.

**Key references from this paper:** Cites "several published general AI competency recommendations for medical students" (refs 10, 11, 14, 22-28), including Lee et al. 2024 below.

---

### 2. Lee et al. 2024 — Medical AI Competencies via Delphi Survey
**Citation:** Lee YM, Kim S, Lee YH, et al. Defining Medical AI Competencies for Medical School Graduates: Outcomes of a Delphi Survey and Medical Student/Educator Questionnaire of South Korean Medical Schools. *Academic Medicine*. 2024;99(5):524-533. PMID: 38207056.

**What it does:** Used a Delphi survey (n=28 experts, then n=1,955 students/faculty validation) to define 42 competency items across 6 domains for medical graduates.

**WG3 relevance:** Methodological precedent — same Delphi approach WG3 will use. The 42 competency items could inform WG3's question drafting. South Korean context limits direct applicability but the methodology is transferable.

---

### 3. AAMC AI Competencies for Medical Educators (2024-2025)
**Source:** AAMC Advancing AI Resource Collection — developed by CGEA Faculty Development Special Interest Group.

**What it does:** Defines AI competencies specifically for *educators* (not just learners). Mapped to the 2025 International Advisory Committee for AI Vision and Integration Framework. With Josiah Macy Jr. Foundation support, AAMC is also developing national competencies for AI in medical education across the learning continuum.

**WG3 relevance:** Directly addresses faculty development (a WG3 topic). The educator-focused framing is distinctive — most frameworks target learners. The AAMC imprimatur gives it institutional weight.

---

### 4. Tolentino et al. 2024 — Scoping Review of AI Curriculum Frameworks
**Citation:** Tolentino R, Baradaran A, Gore G, Pluye P, Abbasgholizadeh-Rahimi S. Curriculum Frameworks and Educational Programs in AI for Medical Students, Residents, and Practicing Physicians: Scoping Review. *JMIR Med Educ*. 2024;10:e54793. PMID: 39023999.

**What it does:** Systematic review of AI curriculum frameworks and educational programs across the trainee continuum (students, residents, practicing physicians). Found only 2 comprehensive curriculum frameworks and 11 documented real-world programs as of mid-2024.

**WG3 relevance:** Establishes the gap WG3 is trying to fill. Useful for framing the "state of the evidence" in WG3's evidence brief and the AEM manuscript. Also covers residents and practicing physicians — broader scope than most frameworks.

---

### 5. AI-PACE Framework (2026)
**Source:** Published on arXiv (preprint). Organized around 4 pillars grounded in Bloom's Taxonomy.

**WG3 relevance:** Most recent framework. Uses a pedagogical structure (Bloom's) that may resonate with education-focused WG3 members. Preprint status means it hasn't been peer-reviewed.

---

### 6. AIFM-ed Curriculum Framework (2025)
**Citation:** Published in *JMIR Med Educ*. 2025;e66828. Mixed methods study for postgraduate family medicine education.

**WG3 relevance:** One of the few frameworks targeting *postgraduate* education specifically. Relevant to WG3's GME scope, though family medicine context differs from EM.

---

### 7. AMEE Guide No. 178 (2025)
**Source:** Masters K, MacNeil H. Artificial Intelligence in Health Professions Education Assessment. *Medical Teacher*. 2025.

**WG3 relevance:** Focuses specifically on *assessment* of AI competencies — a gap most other frameworks don't address. Assessment is one of WG3's suggested topics ("strategies for teaching clinicians to critically appraise AI outputs").

## What's Missing — WG3's Unique Contribution

None of these frameworks:

1. **Address emergency medicine specifically.** EM has unique AI applications (triage, time-critical decision support, high-throughput workflows) that create distinct competency needs.

2. **Span the full career continuum in one framework.** Most target UME or a single stage. WG3's scope includes UME, GME, practicing clinicians, and faculty — requiring a unified framework or deliberate articulation across stages.

3. **Define how to keep training current** as AI capabilities evolve rapidly. WG3's topic list includes "training that keeps pace with the technology" — a process/infrastructure question, not just a content question.

4. **Establish research priorities** for AI education. The existing frameworks are primarily *prescriptive* (here's what to teach). WG3's Delphi output should be *generative* — identifying the highest-priority research questions about how to teach, assess, and sustain AI competency.
