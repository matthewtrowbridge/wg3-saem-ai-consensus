What should every emergency physician know about practicing alongside AI?

The conference

SAEM AI Consensus 2026

SAEM's 2026 Consensus Conference on Artificial Intelligence in Emergency Medicine brings together five working groups on the eve of SAEM25 to produce an evidence-grounded, multi-stakeholder agenda. Funded by AHRQ R13.

WG3's charge

Education, training & competency

We map directly to Aim 3 of the R13: identify competencies and curricular anchors that prepare EM clinicians — trainees and attendings, academic and community — for AI-integrated practice. Scope includes UME, GME, CME, and assessment.

Our approach

Scenarios-first, lightly Delphic

Rather than a formal multi-round Delphi, WG3 is starting from concrete EM clinical scenarios where AI is already deployed and working backwards to the competencies they demand — then stress-testing against existing frameworks and the Makutonin prior themes.

Where the work is heading

Near-term goal: a defensible straw-man scenarios × themes framework ready for group review at the WG3 pre-conference meeting, so we converge on substantive content — not scope — when we get to Atlanta.

1 · Ground the framework in real EM work

Carl Preiksaitis has assembled 14 concrete clinical vignettes in which EM clinicians are already interacting with AI. Each becomes an anchor for competency discussion.

3 · Identify gaps

Where scenarios don't land cleanly on an existing theme — alert fatigue, wearables, GOC ethics, interprofessional use, cognitive autonomy — we surface candidate new themes for discussion.
