Working Group 3

What should every emergency physician know about practicing alongside AI?

WG3 is building the first SAEM consensus on the competencies, curricular anchors, and assessment strategies that emergency medicine education needs as AI tools enter the clinical workflow. Our output feeds the May 21 conference, a dedicated AEM Education & Training manuscript on AI competency gaps, and contributes to the broader AEM proceedings paper.

WG3's charge

Education, training & competency

We map directly to Aim 3 of the R13: identify competencies and curricular anchors that prepare EM clinicians — trainees and attendings, academic and community — for AI-integrated practice. Scope includes UME, GME, CME, and assessment.

AHRQ R13 overview →

Our approach

Scenarios-first, lightly Delphic

Rather than a formal multi-round Delphi, WG3 is starting from concrete EM clinical scenarios where AI is already deployed and working backwards to the competencies they demand — then stress-testing against existing frameworks and the SAEM25 nominal group themes.

Where the work is heading

Near-term goal: a defensible straw-man scenarios × themes framework ready for group review at the WG3 pre-conference meeting, so we converge on substantive content — not scope — when we get to Atlanta.

1 · Ground the framework in real EM work

Carl Preiksaitis has assembled 14 concrete clinical vignettes in which EM clinicians are already interacting with AI. Each becomes an anchor for competency discussion.

Browse the 14 scenarios →

2 · Map them to prior themes

The SAEM25 nominal group produced four themes (Communicating · Use Cases · Interacting · Medico-legal) and ten subthemes. We test their coverage against the scenarios.

See the theme set →

3 · Identify gaps

Where scenarios don't land cleanly on an existing theme — alert fatigue, wearables, GOC ethics, interprofessional use, cognitive autonomy — we surface candidate new themes for discussion.

Candidate gap themes →

4 · Converge on a publishable structure

Output: a themed competency framework + assessment sketch for AEM / AEM E&T, positioned alongside Triola & Rodman (2025), the AAMC, and Lee et al. (2024).
