Scenarios × Themes — discussion framework

Each column is a theme (or subtheme) from the SAEM25 nominal group. Each row is one of Carl's 14 clinical scenarios. Scores are a provisional 0–3 strength estimate — meant to be argued with, not ratified. Hover any theme code for its full definition; click a row for the scenario's theme mapping in detail.

Where these themes come from. The column headers in this matrix are drawn from a nominal group exercise conducted at SAEM25 by a group of EM faculty in informatics and AI research. That session — a one-hour structured brainstorm — produced four themes and ten subthemes through sticky-note clustering and transcript coding. A planned Delphi validation round was not completed. WG3's task is to complete that validation: stress-test the themes against concrete clinical scenarios, surface gaps, and produce a publishable consensus framework.

Discussion prompts for the WG3 meeting

  1. Do the existing four themes still cover the territory once stress-tested against 14 concrete EM scenarios? Where do they strain?
  2. Which of the candidate new themes deserve first-class status vs. subtheme status vs. being folded into existing themes?
  3. Which scenarios are most urgent to teach now, vs. which can wait for v2 of the framework?
  4. Does "communicating about AI" need to split into patient-facing and team-facing competencies?
  5. What's missing entirely? Ambient scribing, patient-brought-AI, and metacognition came up on the kickoff call but aren't on Carl's 14.
  6. Does the academic / community persona dimension change the competency weights for any scenario?
  7. Where does the framework overlap with WG4 (Training) — and what's the explicit hand-off?
  8. New (Apr 10): Should competency weights shift based on professional persona — community ED physician (functioning safely with deployed tools) vs. academic clinician-educator (teaching others to evaluate AI) vs. academic researcher (shaping and evaluating new AI tools)? If so, does the framework need a persona axis in addition to the scenario × theme matrix?
